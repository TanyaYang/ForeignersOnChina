INTRODUCTION

As they become more and more popular on social meida sites, short videosdeo have connectd people around the world to know each other better. Their footage reflects real storeis about China’s development and life of the Chinese people.

Given by this, we want to collect short multilingual videos in the form of story pieces, talk shows, viral social media videos and others, with the theme of China opening up to the world, China going global, its urban construction, its global brands and the spreading of Chinese culture, from video creators, we-media hosts and bloggers around the world, both professional and amateurs are welcomed. We aim to encourage people around the world who are interested in China to show their unique and fresh ideas about China, to tell stories between China and the world based on their own understanding, to comment on modern Chinese people and Chinese society and to elucidate their thoughts about China’s reform and development.

Outstanding videos will be widely spread by seven international communication medias covering 10 languages, including China.org.cn, CIPG Digital Media Center, Beijing Review, China Pictorial, China Today, People’s China, China Report under the China International Publishing Group to mobile internet users around the world through platforms such as social medias and portal websites.

CATEGORIES

Entries, with the theme of China and the world, should tell stories like Chinese enterprises going global, foreigners living, travelling or starting a business in China, transnational enterprises operating in China, Chinese people studying, starting a businesses and living abroad ,and China’s reform and development from a individual’s stroy, and give objective comments towards the trend of Chinese society and so on, to help overseas audience know China in a real and comprehensive way.

All entries will be classified into 5 categories according to their own themes.

Unit One. Sensing China around the world

Stories about China around the world

Tell stories about China in your city in your own way. Stories about Chinese enterprises, Chinese people, Chinese food and Chinese products and so on are all what we expected.

This unit is for participants from the whole world, especially for overseas Chinese and institutions functioning abroad.

Unit Two. Living in China and witnessing its development

Ideas and feelings of foreigners to the current Chinese economy, internet and the development of technology.

As a foreigner in China, what does China’s booming economy mean for you? How do you feel when you see all these endless new things in China, including the high-speed railway, new energy automobiles, the green architecture, shared bikes, unmanned supermarkets and mobile payment? Share with us the new-tech stuff in China that you love, participated, or are eager to tell and we can help you let it be known in the world.

This unit is for foreigners who work and live or have worked and lived in China.

Unit Three. The Chinese city or cities I love the most

Feelings, ideas and expectations to Chinese cities of foreigners.

It may be an encounter between you and the Chinese city you love, or you came there just for your dream. Stories happen every day. In which city are you living now? Or have you stayed? And which is your favourite one? We expect you to tell the story of Chinese cities, big or small, to tell us why you love it, what makes it special, what kind of culture or history it has, how Chinese people there live their life, what kind of future you expect it could be. Your video can tell us the uniqueness of the city.

This unit is for foreigners who work and live or have worked and lived in China.

Stories between me and Chinese people.

Tell or film stories between you and Chinese people in your vision as a foreigner, which may advance the communication between Chinese and people from outside of China.

Your experiences of working with or simply encountering Chinese people must be very interesting. How do Chinese view their life, work? What is their personality? What do they think of the world and the things around them? How do they behave? What kind of differences do you and Chinese have? And we hope more people could know the values of Chinese people and the ways how they think and solve problems through your videos.

This unit is for foreigners who work and live or have worked and lived in China.

Unit Five. Foreigner in China

Stories about foreigners starting business, working, living and traveling in China.

This part is about only YOU. Your experiences about your starting your own business, working, living and traveling is the core content in this video. We are looking forward to your sharing.

This unit is for all Chinese and foreigners, but the key roles should be non-Chinses citizens.
